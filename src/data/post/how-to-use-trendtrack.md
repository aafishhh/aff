## How to Use TrendTrack for Product Research

Product research is where most users spend their time, and it is where TrendTrack's store-first approach shines compared to ad-only tools like Minea or PiPiAds.

### Finding Trending Shops

The Trending Shops tab is your starting point. It displays Shopify stores ranked by growth momentum — not just ad activity, but actual traffic and revenue signals.

#### Filter Strategies for Trending Shops

The filters available here are more granular than any competitor offers. Here are three filter combinations that consistently surface high-potential opportunities:

**Filter Set 1 — New Brands Scaling Fast**

- Store creation date: Last 6 months
- Traffic growth: >50% in last 30 days
- Active ads: >10
- Category: (your niche)

This combination finds stores that launched recently and are already gaining significant traction. A brand created three months ago with 80K monthly traffic and 30+ active ads is almost certainly running a validated, profitable product.

**Filter Set 2 — Established Brands Pushing Hard**

- Traffic range: 100K–500K monthly visits
- Ad count: >100 active ads
- Ad growth: >30% in last 90 days
- EU ad spend (Pro plan): >$5,000/month

This set surfaces mature stores that are aggressively scaling ad spend. Their creatives are battle-tested, and their landing pages are optimized. Study them not to copy, but to reverse-engineer what works.

**Filter Set 3 — Niche Discovery**

- Category: (explore a category you haven't researched before)
- Product count: <50 (focused catalogs)
- Traffic growth: >20% in last 30 days
- Shopify apps: includes Loox or Judge.me (indicates review-driven products)

Small catalogs with growing traffic often indicate a single hero product driving the business. These are the cleanest signals for product validation.

### Analyzing Store Intelligence

Once you find an interesting store, click into its profile. Here is what matters and why:

#### What to Look for in Store Data

**Traffic trajectory** — A store with 20K monthly traffic six months ago and 120K today is in a steep growth phase. That growth curve tells you something is working. A flat or declining traffic line means the store's products may be saturated.

**Top products** — TrendTrack shows each store's best-selling items. If a store runs 200 ads but 80% of its traffic goes to a single product page, that product is the winner. Everything else is testing.

**Tech stack** — The apps a store uses reveal its operational maturity. Klaviyo for email marketing, Loox for photo reviews, ReCharge for subscriptions, PageFly for custom landing pages — each app signals a specific growth strategy. When you see the same app combination across multiple successful stores in your niche, it is a strong signal that those tools support that business model.

**Pixel configuration** — A store running Facebook Pixel, TikTok Pixel, and Google Ads simultaneously is either very profitable (supporting multi-platform ad spend) or operated by an experienced team. Both are useful signals.

When you learn [how to use TrendTrack](/) for this kind of store-level analysis, you stop guessing and start making decisions based on how successful operators actually run their businesses.

## How to Use TrendTrack for Ad Intelligence

### Using Trending Ads

The Trending Ads tab is TrendTrack's ad library — a searchable database of active Meta ads with rich metadata. Unlike the Meta Ad Library (which only shows ad creative and run dates), TrendTrack adds store context, ad spend estimates, and engagement signals.

#### Ad Filters That Actually Matter

**Days running** — Ads that have been active for 20+ days are almost certainly profitable. Nobody runs an unprofitable ad for three weeks. Filter for 20+ days to find validated winners.

**Ad format** — Filter by video for UGC-style ads (the dominant format in 2026), or by image for traditional DTC brand ads. Video ads with high engagement are your best source of hook and angle inspiration.

**Category** — Narrow to your niche to avoid noise. If you sell pet products, filter to "Pets" and skip the beauty and fitness ads that would otherwise clutter your feed.

**EU ad spend (Pro plan)** — This is TrendTrack's exclusive feature. Real disclosed ad spend from EU/UK transparency regulations. Filter by minimum spend to find ads backed by serious budgets — if a brand is spending $10,000+/month on a single ad, the creative is working.

### Using the BrandTracker

BrandTracker is where competitive intelligence becomes systematic. Instead of manually searching for a competitor's ads every few days, you add them to your tracker and get a daily-updated dashboard.

#### Setting Up Competitor Monitoring

**Step 1** — From Trending Shops or Search, find your competitor's store profile.

**Step 2** — Click "Track" to add them to your BrandTracker. The Starter plan allows 2 tracked brands, Pro allows 30, and Business is unlimited.

**Step 3** — Organize tracked brands into three groups:

- **Direct competitors** — stores selling similar products at similar price points
- **Indirect competitors** — stores targeting your audience with different products
- **Aspirational brands** — successful stores you want to learn from

**Step 4** — Review your BrandTracker dashboard weekly. For each tracked brand, you will see:

- New ads launched since your last check (the testing feed)
- Ad duration trends (are they running longer or shorter?)
- Landing page changes
- Creative format shifts (did they switch from image to video?)
- Headline and copy variations with run-time data

The testing feed is particularly valuable. When a competitor launches 5 new ads in a week, they are testing a new angle or product. When they kill an ad that ran for 30 days, something changed — maybe the product saturated, maybe the creative fatigued, maybe they found a better variant. These signals tell you what is happening in your market right now.

### Mistake 3: Not Saving Filter Presets

If you run the same filter combination every week, save it as a Smart Segment. TrendTrack remembers your saved filters, so you can run your entire research workflow with one click instead of manually configuring filters each time. Users who skip this step waste 10–15 minutes per session re-entering the same parameters.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring BrandTracker for Long-Term Strategy

Trending Shops and Trending Ads are great for discovery, but BrandTracker is where competitive intelligence compounds over time. Without tracking specific competitors, you have no historical context — you can't tell if a competitor's new ad is a one-off test or part of a systematic scaling push. Add your top 5–10 competitors to BrandTracker in week one and let the data accumulate.

## Advanced Workflow: Building a Weekly Research Routine

The best way to master how to use TrendTrack is to build a repeatable weekly routine. Here is a framework that takes about 90 minutes per week and covers discovery, analysis, and creative strategy.

### Monday: Trend Scan (30 minutes)

Open Trending Shops with your saved filter presets. Look for:

- New stores that appeared since last week
- Traffic spikes on existing tracked stores
- Category shifts (is a new niche gaining momentum?)

Add any interesting new stores to your BrandTracker.

### Wednesday: Competitor Deep Dive (30 minutes)

Review your BrandTracker dashboard. For each tracked competitor:

- Check the testing feed for new ads launched since Monday
- Note which ads were killed (run ended) — this signals fatigue or failure
- Review any landing page changes
- Save notable creatives to your swipe file folders

### Friday: Creative Review (30 minutes)

Open Trending Ads and filter for:

- Ads running 20+ days in your niche (validated winners)
- New video ads launched this week (fresh creative angles)
- High-spend EU ads (Pro plan) — these are backed by real budgets

Save 5–10 standout creatives. For each, write a one-line note on what makes it work: the hook, the angle, the offer, the visual format.

Over a month, this routine builds a library of validated product opportunities, competitor movement patterns, and creative inspiration — all grounded in data, not guesswork.

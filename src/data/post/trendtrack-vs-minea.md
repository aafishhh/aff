**TrendTrack** answers: *"Which Shopify stores are growing, and what are they doing to get there?"*

This is not a minor distinction. It changes what you can discover, how you work, and what decisions you can make.

## Store Intelligence: Where TrendTrack Pulls Ahead

This is TrendTrack's biggest structural advantage and Minea's biggest gap.

TrendTrack maintains a database of over **10,000 Shopify stores** with rich metadata for each:

- **Traffic estimates** — monthly visit ranges and growth trends
- **Top products** — best-selling items per store
- **Tech stack detection** — Shopify apps, themes, pixels, and tracking tools
- **Store metadata** — creation date, market, language, currency, Trustpilot score
- **Ad count** — how many active ads each store is running

You can filter stores by traffic range, traffic growth rate, ad count, category, product count, price range, Shopify apps, and more. This lets you answer questions that Minea simply cannot:

- *"Which stores had a traffic spike in the last 7 days?"*
- *"Which stores use Klaviyo + Loox reviews and run more than 10 Meta ads?"*
- *"Which stores in the home & garden niche are scaling fastest?"*

Minea has no equivalent. Its store research module is limited to identifying stores that run ads — it does not provide traffic data, growth trends, tech stack detection, or the deep filtering that makes TrendTrack's store intelligence actionable.

### The Store-to-Ad Pipeline

TrendTrack's real power is connecting store signals with ad data. When you find a store with a traffic spike, you can immediately see its active Meta ads, its top products, and its landing pages — all in one workflow. Minea cannot do this because it lacks the store intelligence layer.

For dropshippers and brand owners, this pipeline is the difference between *"I found a cool ad"* and *"I found a store that is actively scaling, here is exactly how."*

## Ad Coverage: Where Minea Has an Edge

Minea wins on raw ad platform coverage. Its database includes ads from **four platforms**:

1. **Facebook** — full coverage
2. **Instagram** — full coverage
3. **TikTok** — full coverage with filters for likes, comments, shares, country, language, and e-commerce type
4. **Pinterest** — full coverage (this is unique to Minea; no other major spy tool covers Pinterest ads)

TrendTrack covers **Facebook and Instagram** through the Meta Ads Library, with limited TikTok coverage and no Pinterest at all.

If your marketing strategy heavily involves Pinterest (which is relevant for home decor, fashion, beauty, and DIY niches), Minea is the only tool that gives you Pinterest ad intelligence. That is a genuine, non-trivial advantage.

Similarly, if TikTok ads are central to your strategy, Minea provides dedicated TikTok ad spying with engagement filters. TrendTrack's TikTok coverage is acknowledged as limited — it is not the platform's core strength.

### Ad Database Size

Minea's ad database is significantly larger: **900M+ ads** versus TrendTrack's **10M+ ads**. However, raw database size is not the same as data quality. Minea indexes more ads across more platforms, but TrendTrack's smaller database is enriched with store-level context (traffic, growth, revenue signals) that Minea's ads lack.

The right question is not *"which has more ads?"* but *"which helps me make better decisions?"* — and that depends on whether you need platform breadth (Minea) or store-level depth (TrendTrack).

## Real-World Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Dropshipper Finding Winning Products

**With Minea:** You browse the ad library, filter by platform and engagement metrics, manually evaluate each ad, and decide if the product is worth testing. This works, but it requires hours of manual browsing and relies on your judgment to separate real winners from noise.

**With TrendTrack:** You browse Shopify stores that had traffic spikes this week, see their top products and active ads, check the EU ad spend data to confirm the ad is actually backed by real budget, and add the store to your Brand Tracker for ongoing monitoring. You can also ask Claude via MCP: *"Show me stores in the fitness niche that started running new Meta ads in the last 7 days and had a traffic increase."*

### Use Case 2: Brand Owner Monitoring Competitors

**With Minea:** You search for your competitor's name in the ad library, browse their ads, and manually track changes over time. There is no automated monitoring or historical timeline.

**With TrendTrack:** You add competitors to your Brand Tracker. Every day, you get a testing feed showing their new ads, a timeline of creative changes, their top landing pages, video transcripts, and headline/copy variant tables. You see not just what they are running, but how long each variant has been running and which ones get copied most — revealing their highest-performing angles.

### Use Case 3: Ad Agency Managing Multiple Clients

**With Minea:** You manually search the ad library for each client's competitors. There is no team structure, no multi-client workflow, and no centralized dashboard.

**With TrendTrack:** The Business plan supports 4 users and unlimited brand trackers. You set up brand trackers for each client's competitors, and the whole team has access to daily monitoring feeds. EU ad spend data lets you demonstrate concrete ROI benchmarks in client reports.

### Use Case 4: TikTok-First Dropshipper

**With Minea:** You get full TikTok ad coverage with engagement filters (likes, comments, shares, country, language, e-commerce type). This is Minea's strength for TikTok-first sellers.

**With TrendTrack:** TikTok coverage is limited. If TikTok is your primary and only ad platform, Minea is the better choice for this specific use case.

### Use Case 5: Pinterest-First Seller (Home Decor, Fashion)

**With Minea:** You get Pinterest ad coverage — the only spy tool that offers this. If Pinterest is central to your marketing, Minea has no alternative.

**With TrendTrack:** No Pinterest coverage. You would need to supplement with another tool or rely on Pinterest's native ad library.
